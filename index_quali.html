    About the project:

    This project aims to visualize the quality of coffee, both Arabica and Robusta species, around the world in 6 different aspects:

    • Aroma: Refers to the smell or fragrance of the coffee.
    • Flavor: Evaluated based on the taste, including any sweetness, bitterness, acidity, and other flavor notes.
    • Aftertaste: Refers to the lingering taste that remains in the mouth after swallowing the coffee.
    • Acidity: Pertains to the liveliness or sharpness of the flavor.
    • Balance: Concerns how well the different components of the coffee"s flavor work together.
    • Sweetness: Relates to the range of sweetness offered by its flavor.

    Thus, a coffee lover like us can better evaluate which package of coffee to take off the shelves!

    The data was collected from the Coffee Quality Institute (CQI) and the Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) and the visualization was developed using D3.js.

    About the data:

    The Coffee Quality Institute (CQI) maintains an online database that serves as a resource for coffee professionals and enthusiasts interested in learning about coffee quality and sustainability. The database includes a variety of information on coffee production, processing, and sensory evaluation. It also contains data on coffee genetics, soil types, and other factors that can affect coffee quality.

    The dataset is available at Kaggle. The set was adapted where the score for each aspect ranges from 0 to 10 and was modified to range from 0 to 5, for better interpretation. We use 'avg' to indicate when the value refers to the average of some selection of the data.
